Concept

Our team came up with this project because we all had similar interest in foot wear and street wear. After observing the sneakers and streetwear market we came up with a concept that can provide "hype" items for buyers at a reasonable price.

Competitive Analysis

We then performed competitive analysis on popular brand's store and mobile application as well as popular resell platforms. Adidas's confirmed app, Nike's SNKRS as well as popular resell platform such as StockX and GOAT.

  • StockX/GOAT: current go-to sites for many hypebeasts. Safe way to buy/sell shoes but a lot of cundercutting by sellers, high fee costs, high buyer shopping prices
  • Ebay: traditional oldschool way to buy/sell items personally. In recent years fakes have become an issue and ebay not the most trusted site in selling/buying authentic goods
  • Facebook marketplace, letgo/offerup: can avoid high fees but limited to local users so smaller coverage of potential buyers and sellers
  • Adidas/SNKRS: Retailer's own platform that showcases their current items. Due to the high demand on certain limited release these apps ususally use methods such as random release time and "lottery" to sell their products.

Prototype

Boosted went through many different iterations. At Boosted our goal is to bridge the gap between the stalemate often going on between “high ball” offers of sellers and “low ball” offers of buyers and speed up the frequency of transactions.Sellers don’t have to wait extended periods for their items to sell and buyers can place a low risk investment on an item they otherwise couldnt afford paying resale for.

To achieve this we devised a game like lottery format. Seller post their items on our platform, prices ending in 0 would be ideal. Buyers can then purchase $5 or $10 game entries to participate in that sale. This will continue until all slots are filled up for that item. Each particiant then plays a game and whoever has the highest score or fastesst time will get the item.

Although the game part of the platform is still undetermined and since lottery would be illegal we looked to incorporate concepts used popular competitive mobile gaming platform "Skillz".
